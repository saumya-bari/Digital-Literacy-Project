## Part A: Case Study – Phishing  
### The Hook, Line, and Sinker of Digital Deception

Phishing is a cybercrime technique where attackers impersonate trusted organizations—such as banks, universities, or popular platforms—to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like usernames, passwords, OTPs, or banking details. It relies on psychological manipulation rather than technical hacking.

### How it Happens: A Realistic Scenario  

1. **The Lure:**  
A student receives an email, SMS (smishing), or call (vishing) that appears to come from their university, bank, or platforms like Amazon or Paytm.

2. **Creating Urgency or Fear:**  
The message claims:
- “Your account will be suspended”
- “Suspicious login detected”
- “Pending fee payment required”

This creates panic and forces quick action.

3. **The Hook:**  
The message contains a malicious link or attachment. Clicking it redirects the user to a fake website that looks identical to the real one.

4. **The Landing:**  
The spoofed website has a slightly altered URL (e.g., spelling errors or extra characters).

5. **The Catch:**  
The user enters login credentials or OTP, which are instantly captured by the attacker.

6. **Post-Exploitation:**  
Attackers may access accounts, transfer money, reuse credentials on other platforms, or sell data online.

### Target and Consequences  

**Targets:**
- College students  
- First-time digital users  
- People under stress  

**Consequences:**
- Financial loss  
- Identity theft  
- Account hacking  
- Data breaches  
- Emotional stress  

Phishing is dangerous because it looks genuine and exploits human trust.
